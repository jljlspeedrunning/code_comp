What are we doing to sustain our water and other resources?

With an ever-increasing population and climate change threatening to stop that, many ecosystems have been damaged from the decrease in native population of the environment. Droughts, floods, and storms have become more common than before. Various reforms include a more proper management of water usage and its impact on the environment, flood and storm management, and government policies to control water management.

Of course, government intervention itself won't do much. What else is going on?

Scientists had already come up with filtering salt from ocean water, treating wastewater, and drilling for groundwater. While these aquifers are used in many places, they are running out faster than they are being filled, so they are not long-term solutions. Many long-term solutions require a lot of power, so without efficient and reliable power sources, water cannot be treated as reliably. Some places consider a solar-powered desalination plant the best option for water treatment.
